Education · Language · Web App
Genki Dojo
"One app. Everything you need for Genki I."
A free, open-source study companion for Genki I Japanese learners. All 12 lessons — grammar, vocabulary, kana drills, and speaking practice — offline-ready. No login. No cost.
Problem Genki I learners had no single, free, offline-capable study tool — just scattered PDFs and paywalled apps.
Outcome All 12 lessons, zero cost, installable offline — a full course in one app used by learners internationally.

Classroom Kitchen
"No student goes hungry today."
A delivery-first food service built for schools. Full UX process — personas, sitemap, wireframes, mood board, and a working deployed site. Hot bento meals ordered directly to classrooms in seconds.
Problem Students losing class time navigating complex lunch systems — or going without because the process was too hard.
Outcome End-to-end UX from research to deployed site — food ordering reduced to a 3-step flow with full Behance case study.

About me

Bianca Dene Williams
Teacher · Designer · Builder
CalArts Web Design Certificate · 2026
↗ Behance ↗ GitHub ↗ LinkedIn ↗ bianca.dene.williams@gmail.com

I have been teaching English as a Second Language for over fifteen years. Language learning sits at the intersection of empathy, patience, and design. You are constantly finding new ways to make something click for someone who sees the world differently than you do. That experience shaped everything that came after.

My path into design and development started at the University of Arizona, where I worked in the campus multimedia labs. I needed to learn these skills to build things for the work I was doing and to support students with their own projects. What started as necessity became something I could not stop. I kept building.

Over the years I have worked across education, UX, and front-end development, often all three at once. The projects here reflect that range: a Japanese study app built because the right tool did not exist, a school meal service born from watching hungry kids try to learn, a maternal health concept for the women nobody designs for. Every idea came from something real.

I completed the CalArts Web Design Certificate Program in 2026. I build in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, and Figma. I work independently, move fast, and care deeply about the people on the other side of whatever I make.

Currently exploring: design systems, accessibility auditing, and motion design for interfaces.
